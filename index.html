About Solfezz

From Junior Dev to Lead Engineer

Solfège gave musicians a universal language to navigate between the abstract world of music theory and the deeply human act of making sound. It worked in both directions — and that was the point.

Solfezz borrows that same philosophy and brings it into technology. The work is always bidirectional. In one direction — taking the chaotic, the overwhelming, the abstract, and distilling it into clean architecture that anyone can build on. In the other — taking rigid, mechanical systems and giving them rhythm, insight, and a reason to exist beyond their function.

That tension between abstraction and structure, between clarity and soul, is where the best work lives. It is not just how we build. It is how we think.

Solfezz is the dev brand, a full stack developer based in Riverside County, CA with over 10 years of experience building enterprise systems that contributed directly to growing a company from $2–3M to nearly $30M in annual revenue. Prior experience includes supporting 650+ automotive dealership websites on a C# .NET platform.

Most work was built for proprietary internal systems and is not publicly available. This portfolio describes the systems built, problems solved, and outcomes delivered. Work samples and references available upon request.

The Solfezz Lens

Philosophy in Practice

Every project moves in one of two directions — or both at once. Here are three that best exemplify what Solfezz means in the real world: what the system was, what it became, and which direction it moved in.

Direction One
The ERP Migration
Legacy PHP → Modular MVC Architecture
⟶

A sprawling, organically grown codebase — years of logic tangled together with no shared language, no structure, no way for anyone to see the whole picture. The chaos was real. But so was the pattern hiding inside it.

PHPLaravel CodeIgniterModular MVC
Abstract → Universal
Finding the pattern inside the chaos
The business had not changed. The problems had not changed. But the code had become a language only its author could read — abstract in the worst sense. The work was to look past the noise and find the underlying logic that could be generalized, structured, and handed to anyone.

The result was a modular MVC architecture that transformed accumulated complexity into a system with a shared grammar. New modules could be built. Old ones could be understood. The abstract became universal — and the company scaled 10x on top of it.
"The code was not the problem. The lack of a shared language was. Solfezz found the pattern inside the chaos and gave it a shape everyone could read."
Rigid → Alive
Giving a mechanical process a pulse
A warehouse hiring form is one of the most sterile objects in business. Fixed fields. Binary outcomes. No signal beyond what someone writes about themselves. It is rigid in every direction — and it tells you almost nothing about how a person actually thinks.

The work was to take that concrete structure and breathe something into it. The result was a Connect 4 game that revealed decision-making, pattern recognition, and composure under pressure — things no form could ever surface. The goal was identical. The experience became entirely different.
"A hiring form tells you nothing. A game tells you everything. Solfezz took the rigidity of recruitment and gave it a pulse."
Direction Two
The Hiring Assessment
Warehouse Screening → Connect 4 Game
⟵

A rigid HR screening process with a fixed outcome — turned into an interactive game that revealed how candidates actually think. Same goal. Completely different experience. Used to evaluate 50+ warehouse candidates.

PHPJavaScript jQueryHTML/CSS
Abstract → Universal
Sound became searchable structure
A voicemail is one of the most abstract inputs a business can receive — unstructured, ephemeral, impossible to act on at scale. You cannot search it, assign it, track it, or measure it. It exists and then disappears.

Google Cloud Speech-to-Text distilled that raw audio into clean text that could be logged, routed, assigned, and tracked inside a unified customer service system. The abstract became universal — actionable by anyone, visible to everyone.
"Sound became text. The ephemeral became a managed record. The abstract was universalized in a single API call."
Both Directions
Voicemail Transcription Module
The purest expression of Solfezz
⟷

One module. One missed call. Two directions at once — the abstract universalized, the rigid humanized. A missed moment transformed into a managed conversation.

Google Cloud Gmail API PHP Vue.js
Rigid → Alive
A missed call became a human moment
A missed phone call is one of the coldest, most transactional moments in customer service. It has no context, no warmth, no path forward. The rigid process says: call back when you can. That is all.

The module transformed that moment into something a customer service agent could read, understand, empathize with, and respond to thoughtfully. The mechanical act of missing a call became an opportunity for genuine connection. The rigid became alive — one transcribed message at a time.
"A missed call became a managed conversation. The cold became warm. The rigid was given soul — one transcribed message at a time."
